Our Mission

The Southeast Community Development Corporation (SCDC) is a nonprofit 501(c)3 organization established in 1994 as a regional community and economic development agency. The mission of SCDC is to promote community health, education, and safety, provide economic development, and improve the living standard for southeast Los Angeles County residents.

Transparency

Basic computer and technology literacy classes with a hands on approach for websites, digital menus, and overall digital and tech literacy training. These classes create a welcoming and creative environment for small business oweners who are not well equipped or have little to no knowledge about technology and digital literacy.

Topics Covered in class

  • Professional websites
  • Staff training on digital literacy skills
  • Digital Menus

A recent community assessment report by the University of California, Berkeley indicates that residents of the Southeast areas of Los Angeles County remain largely disconnected from the Internet due to the lack of broad public access to computers in public locations, lack of technical assistance, and lack of computer literacy education. As such, the members of these communities are victims of the Digital Divide, and suffer from a lack of access to information and communications technology, as well as a lack of acquisition of the skills necessary to take full advantage of these technologies for the purposes of education, economic development and citizenship.

Digital Literacy

Small businesses in Southeast Los Angeles should engage with the Southeast Community Development Corporation's digital services because their expertise in providing low-cost digital services and training can contribute to the success of entrepreneurs in the region.

It is very important for smaller business, new and old, to establish some kind of digitl and technological baseline for the growth and longevity of the business. Digital literacy opens up many opportunities for the business through sponsorships, promotions, and advertisements. It also alows the business owners a means to engage with thier customers and get constructive feedback on how they could improve the overall business.

Target Audience

Owners of small businesses include mom-and-pop shops, restaurants, street vendors, salons, repair shops, bakeries, dry cleaners, ice cream shops, office services, etc. The demographics include: Low-income entrepreneurs Age range: 40+ Spanish speakers

The Southeast Cities Technology Collaborative, under the management of the Southeast Community Development Corporation, will conduct a three year technology initiative designed to: Expand community broadband access points Provide computer literacy training Enhance existing community service programs throughout the Southeast region of Los Angeles County
